Nonverbal communication comes in many forms. In fact, there are entire books dedicated to the hundreds of techniques people use without saying a single word. It’s a fascinating area of study, and one I personally look forward to exploring more deeply.

Most of us rely on nonverbal cues without even realizing it. We notice when someone’s facial expression shifts, signaling that we may have lost their attention. We can tell when a person misunderstood us but hesitates to interrupt. When someone leans back, we often sense they’re seeking more space and may be feeling uncomfortable. These subtle signals guide our conversations, and it’s our job as communicators to tune into them while we speak and listen.

For some, nonverbal communication is more than an added layer—it’s a lifeline. Individuals who have experienced a stroke and live with aphasia, a language disorder that affects speaking, understanding, reading, and writing, may rely heavily on nonverbal cues. Others with apraxia, a motor planning disorder, may struggle to physically form words. And those with a voice disorder may not always be heard clearly. In these cases, gestures, facial expressions, and other nonverbal signals often carry the weight of the message.

Being able to recognize and use nonverbal communication effectively can make you a much more powerful communicator. Certain movements, positions, and gestures can capture attention, build trust, and create connection without a single spoken word.

Here are 5 key elements to keep in mind:

1. Eye contact
2. Facial expressions
3. Gestures
4. Posture
5. Proximity

When conversations break down, paying attention to these signals can bridge the gap between confusion and understanding. Communication doesn’t always require words—it requires awareness.

The takeaway: The next time you’re in a conversation, try tuning into the silent signals around you. Notice the raised eyebrow, the shift in posture, or the lean of someone’s body. These unspoken cues may tell you more than the words themselves, helping you connect on a deeper, more human level.
