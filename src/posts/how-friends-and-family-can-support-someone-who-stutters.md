**Listening Matters More Than Correcting**

Everyone has had times when they struggle to express themselves. We all know how frustrating it can be to know what you want to say, but can’t put the words together in that moment. How much more frustrating would it be if someone corrected you or cut off your statement in that moment?  Now imagine this happening frequently throughout the day. If you care about someone who stutters, your support can make a world of difference. Here’s how to help create a safe, encouraging space for conversation.

**Do:**

- Maintain natural eye contact
- Give the person time to finish their words without interrupting
- Listen to the content, not the stutter
- Encourage open conversation without pressure

**Don’t:**

- Finish sentences for them unless they ask
- Tell them to “slow down” or “relax” mid-sentence
- Assume they are nervous or unprepared

**Your Encouragement Is Powerful**

People who stutter often face daily challenges that go unseen. Your patience, respect, and genuine interest in their words can boost their confidence far more than you realize. Help with practicing strategies if they ask for assistance. Allow them to practice self-advocacy to gain confidence.

**A Final Thought**

The goal isn’t to remove the stutter—it’s to make communication meaningful, enjoyable, and empowering. Stuttering is simply part of someone’s speaking style, and their voice deserves to be heard in full. Give them the additional time they need to communicate their thoughts. Avoid interrupting them or finishing their sentences. Self-advocacy and confidence are two of the most important pieces of the puzzle.
